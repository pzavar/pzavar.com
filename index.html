AnonNet: Synthetic Medical Data Marketplace

Project Overview

I teamed up with 3 MBAs and a CS master's student to compete in the UCLA Anderson cross-campus innovation challenge and won first place out of 18 teams in the Healthcare track. We created AnonNet, a synthetic data generation platform that artificially mimics the statistical characteristics of raw patient data, providing data holders the opportunity to share the full power of their raw data while under the protection of our government-grade privacy measures.

Process & Contributions

Through three rounds, we delivered a PRD, a comprehensive Business Plan, and a live business pitch in front of a panel of judges and investors. I wrote and presented the sections: Product, Market, and Development & Operations.

Through 40+ informational interviews that my partner, Shaan Mathur, and I conducted with medical researchers, hospital executives, venture capitalists (VCs), and business advisors, we learned that the key insights for improving patient health lie within the vast amount of patient data that medical institutions have collected worldwide. This data, though, is protected health information that should rightfully be kept private. Leveraging GenAI with differential privacy results in new revenue streams in the medical data market while enabling more research to get done.

GrubMatch: Restaurant Decision Made Easy

Project Overview

GrubMatch solves the age-old problem of "Where should we eat?" by creating a Tinder-like swiping interface for restaurant selection that generates matches based on the preferences of all group members. The app helps friends and couples quickly find restaurants they'll all enjoy.

This project was developed during the COVID-19 pandemic to help restaurants attract diners once restrictions lifted, providing an intuitive way for people to discover new restaurants that match everyone's taste preferences.

Key Achievements

  • Built a recommendation algorithm that processes individual preferences to suggest restaurants acceptable to everyone
  • Integrated with Yelp, Google Places, and OpenTable APIs to provide comprehensive restaurant data
  • Created a group feature that allows multiple users to join a "match session" and find common ground
  • Implemented a learning system that improves suggestions based on user choices over time

Technologies Used

React Native Firebase Node.js MongoDB Recommendation Algorithms

My Role

I led the product development effort, created the matching algorithm, and managed a team of 3 developers. I was responsible for the UX design, user testing, and feature prioritization.

By monitoring student participation, emotional responses, and learning patterns, EngageAI provides educators with actionable insights while offering personalized learning experiences to students.

This project was developed as part of the MSIS 522 course at the University of Washington, aiming to address the challenge of maintaining student engagement in both virtual and physical classroom environments.
